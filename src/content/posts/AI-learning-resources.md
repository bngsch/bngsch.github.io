At the core of this foundation, learners need a solid grasp of computer vision (CV) and reinforcement learning (RL). Because deep learning traditionally matured around vision, foundational deep learning principles are exceptionally well-represented in CV curricula. Additionally, RL—particularly policy gradient methods—provides an ideal framework for the domain adaptation of pretrained models. A dedicated course on generative models is also crucial. From this core, learners can navigate into specialized areas like large language models (LLMs) and graph neural networks (GNNs) based on their application needs.

The following are the premier open-access resources available for each domain:

- Computer Vision
    - Stanford CS231n: A classic, comprehensive deep learning course focused on visual recognition.
    <br />[Course Website](https://cs231n.stanford.edu/schedule.html) | [Video Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rOmsNzYBMe0gJY2XS8AQg16)
- Reinforcement Learning
    - UC Berkeley CS285: The gold standard for deep RL, striking an exceptional balance between foundational theory and practical application.
    <br />[Course Website](https://rail.eecs.berkeley.edu/deeprlcourse/) | [Video Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL_iWQOsE6TfVYGEGiAOMaOzzv41Jfm_Ps)
- Deep Generative Models:
    - UC Berkeley CS294-158: An advanced course providing highly comprehensive coverage of modern generative modeling techniques.
    <br />[Course Website](https://sites.google.com/view/berkeley-cs294-158-sp24/home) | [Video Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLwRJQ4m4UJjPIvv4kgBkvu_uygrV3ut_U)
    - MIT IAP 6.S184: A brilliant short-course sequence that provides a clean, unified introduction to diffusion models, cutting through a notoriously confusing field.
    <br /> [Course Website](https://diffusion.csail.mit.edu/2026/index.html) | [Video Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL57nT7tSGAAXwjhDYcxEycx5W7YoSrZyt)
- Large Language Models
    - Stanford CS336: A rigorous deep-dive into modern LLMs, systematically covering the entire pipeline from pre-training to optimization and serving.
    <br /> [Course Website](https://cs336.stanford.edu/) | [Video Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rMqXOcazWaTUHhq-yembLCV)
- Graph Neural Networks
    - Stanford CS224W: An excellent, accessible introduction to analyzing massive graphs and complex networks with ML.
    <br /> [Course Website](https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224w/) | [Video Lectures](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rOP-ImU-O1rYRg2RFxomvFp)
